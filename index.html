About Me

I realized my passion for technology at age sixteen, when I build my first computer. This lead me to pursue a degree in Computer Science at Boston University's College of Arts and Sciences. I have studied a broad variety of Computer Science classes spanning both theory and practical applications including distributed systems, network security, and machine learning. I have professional experience with Information Technology services and Web Development, and look to focus my academics as well as future career work on data security, internet infrastructure, and software engineering. In my free time I like to play soccer, notably as a center defender for the Boston University Men's Club Soccer team, which went undefeated in the regular season of the 2014-2015 academic year. I also like to snowboard, write fiction, and (obviously) code! I have built several systems containing software architecture ranging in complexity from a simple web server to a multi-layered neural network, and am currently working on a parallel computing system modeled after Google's MapReduce protocol to achieve concurrent computation in a distributed system. I am currently looking for full time positions immediately available following 2017 graduation. Please do not hesitate to contact me at rkuro@bu.edu or call me at (617) 997-3829.
